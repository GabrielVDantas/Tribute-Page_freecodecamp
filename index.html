Robert James "Bobby" Fischer

The chess legend who captivated the world

Here's a time line of Bobby Fischer's life:

  • 1943 - Born in Chicago, United States.
  • 1956 - He played the 'Match of the Century' in a Masters tournament, which he won with a 100% success rate, 13 victories in 13 games.
  • 1957 - He won the American championship at the age of 14, which was the first of eight.
  • 1959 - He had his first participation in the Candidates Tournament in Belgrade, but the spot to challenge the world champion went to Mikhail Tal.
  • 1962 - In his second attempt at the Candidates Tournament in Curaçao, Bobby Fischer accused Soviet players of colluding to manipulate results. Though unproven, this led to a format change, but Fischer ultimately withdrew from the tournament.
  • 1971 - He reversed his decision, defeated Mark Taimanov, Bent Larsen, Tigran Petrosian and earned the right to challenge the world champion, Boris Spassky
  • 1971 - Fischer won 7 games in the match against Spassky's 3 and became the world chess champion
  • 1974 - Bobby Fischer announced that he would renounce his title
  • 1992 - After years in reclusion, Fischer had a rematch in Yugoslavia against Spassky, which took place during international sanctions against the Milosevic government. Consequently, the United States threatened to arrest him upon his return
  • 2004 - He was detained in Japan due to his expired American passport. The United States sought his deportation, but Fischer resolved the standoff by gaining Icelandic citizenship
  • 2008 - Robert James Fischer passed away in Iceland at the age of 64.
